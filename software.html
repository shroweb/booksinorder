Common questions

Software FAQs

What is cloud accounting software?

It's bookkeeping software that runs online rather than on your computer. Your bank transactions feed in automatically, receipts can be photographed and uploaded from your phone, and your bookkeeper can work on the same data in real time — no emailing spreadsheets or sending files back and forth.

How is it different from a spreadsheet?

Spreadsheets can work for very simple sole traders, but they don't connect to your bank, they're prone to formula errors, and they make it almost impossible to track changes over time. Cloud accounting creates a searchable audit trail and produces reliable reports automatically. For anyone VAT-registered or preparing for MTD ITSA, spreadsheets aren't a viable long-term solution.

How does access work?

You invite Books In Order as a user at the permission level you choose. You're always in control — you can see everything we do, and access can be removed at any time. You don't hand over the keys; you just open a door.

Will I have to enter everything myself?

No. Bank transactions feed in automatically via the bank connection. Receipts come in via the mobile app or email. The main things you'll be doing day-to-day are approving transactions and capturing receipts as you go — which takes a few minutes rather than an evening at the end of the month.

Which software do you recommend?

Xero, in most cases. It has the strongest bank feed coverage, it's fully MTD-compatible, and it's well-supported. QuickBooks and FreeAgent are both decent alternatives for simpler setups. The right choice depends on your business — it's worth discussing before you sign up to anything.
